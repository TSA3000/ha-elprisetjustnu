# Cities and Municipalities by Electricity Price Area in Sweden

*Last updated: 24 April 2026*

Since 1 November 2011, Sweden has been divided into four electricity price areas (bidding zones) by Svenska kraftnät (the Swedish national grid operator). Electricity prices can vary between areas depending on production, consumption, and transmission capacity in the national grid.

| Price Area | Main City | Region |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| SE1 | Luleå | Northern Norrland |
| SE2 | Sundsvall | Southern Norrland / Northern Svealand |
| SE3 | Stockholm | Southern Svealand / Northern Götaland |
| SE4 | Malmö | Southern Götaland |

---

## How to Find Your Price Area

- Use **Svenska kraftnät's map** at [natomraden.se](https://www.natomraden.se)
- Contact your local grid operator
- Check your electricity bill (sometimes listed as SE1–SE4)

## Background

The division was introduced following a requirement from the European Commission to highlight capacity shortages in the national grid. The north has a surplus of cheap hydropower, while the south has high consumption and reduced production after nuclear plant closures. Bottlenecks in transmission between north and south create price differences between areas, with SE4 typically having the highest prices and SE1 the lowest.
